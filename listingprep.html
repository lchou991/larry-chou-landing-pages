Why we did not
over-renovate

This home still had older major systems, including an aging roof, older HVAC, and a 100 amp panel. With those bigger items still in the picture, a full renovation would have been the wrong move.

The goal was not to overspend chasing a premium the house was unlikely to get credit for. The goal was to reposition it from needs work to clean, move-in ready, and showing well through focused, high-impact prep.

Throughout the home: we painted every surface — interior and exterior. Walls, doors, trim, ceilings, and the full exterior were done. Switches and outlets were replaced, select ceiling fixtures were updated, bulbs were matched for consistent light, and window screens were removed to maximize clarity and natural light.

Why this
works

Buyers decide fast. Most form a strong impression before they have walked the whole house. A home that feels clean, finished, and intentional gets more showings, more offers, and a faster close — not because it was renovated, but because it was prepared well.

The difference between a home that sits and one that goes pending in a week is usually not the price. It is the presentation. Most sellers either do too little or spend money on things buyers do not value. Knowing the difference is the work. The 16-Day Launch is built around that distinction: clear scope, fast execution, nothing wasted.
